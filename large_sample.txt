MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15th, 2026, by and between Meridian Technology Solutions, Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 2200 Market Street, Suite 1400, Philadelphia, PA 19103 ("Provider"), and Cascadia Financial Holdings, LLC, a Washington limited liability company with its principal place of business at 800 Fifth Avenue, Suite 3200, Seattle, WA 98104 ("Client"). Provider and Client are sometimes referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Provider is engaged in the business of providing enterprise software development, cloud infrastructure management, data analytics, cybersecurity consulting, and related technology services;

WHEREAS, Client desires to engage Provider to perform certain technology services in connection with Client's digital transformation initiative, internal systems modernization, and regulatory compliance programs;

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to establish the terms and conditions under which Provider will render such services to Client;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 - DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Affiliate" means any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party, where "control" means the ownership of more than fifty percent (50%) of the voting securities or equivalent ownership interest.

1.2 "Authorized Users" means the employees, contractors, and agents of Client and its Affiliates who are authorized to access and use the Deliverables in accordance with this Agreement.

1.3 "Business Day" means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday, or a day on which banking institutions in New York, New York are authorized or required by law to be closed.

1.4 "Change Order" means a written document signed by both Parties that describes a modification to the scope, schedule, or fees associated with a Statement of Work, substantially in the form attached hereto as Exhibit C.

1.5 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other Party, whether orally, in writing, or by inspection, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

1.6 "Deliverables" means all software, documentation, reports, analyses, designs, specifications, and other work product created by Provider in the performance of services under this Agreement, as more specifically described in each applicable Statement of Work.

1.7 "Effective Date" means January 15th, 2026.

1.8 "Intellectual Property Rights" means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and other intellectual property rights recognized in any jurisdiction worldwide, including all applications and registrations relating thereto.

1.9 "Key Personnel" means those individuals identified in a Statement of Work as being essential to the performance of the services described therein.

1.10 "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, as defined under applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the California Consumer Privacy Act, the General Data Protection Regulation, and the Washington Privacy Act.

1.11 "Service Level Agreement" or "SLA" means the performance standards and metrics set forth in Exhibit B attached hereto, as may be amended from time to time by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

1.12 "Statement of Work" or "SOW" means a document executed by both Parties that describes the specific services to be performed, the Deliverables to be provided, the timeline for performance, and the fees to be paid, substantially in the form attached hereto as Exhibit A.

ARTICLE 2 - SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 Engagement. Client hereby engages Provider, and Provider hereby accepts such engagement, to perform the services described in each Statement of Work executed by both Parties from time to time during the term of this Agreement. Each Statement of Work shall be subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement unless the Statement of Work expressly states otherwise, in which case the terms of the Statement of Work shall prevail with respect to the services described therein.

2.2 Statements of Work. Each Statement of Work shall include, at a minimum: (a) a detailed description of the services to be performed; (b) the Deliverables to be provided; (c) the timeline and milestones for performance; (d) the fees and payment schedule; (e) the acceptance criteria for each Deliverable; (f) the identification of Key Personnel; and (g) any service levels or performance metrics applicable to the services.

2.3 Change Orders. Either Party may request a change to an existing Statement of Work by submitting a written Change Order request to the other Party. No change to a Statement of Work shall be effective unless and until a Change Order has been executed by authorized representatives of both Parties. Provider shall not be obligated to commence work on any proposed change until the applicable Change Order has been fully executed and any required additional fees have been agreed upon.

2.4 Provider Responsibilities. Provider shall: (a) perform all services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards and practices; (b) assign qualified personnel with the skills, training, and experience necessary to perform the services; (c) comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards in the performance of services; (d) maintain all licenses, permits, and certifications required to perform the services; and (e) cooperate with Client and its other service providers as reasonably necessary to ensure the successful delivery of services.

2.5 Client Responsibilities. Client shall: (a) provide Provider with timely access to Client's facilities, systems, data, and personnel as reasonably necessary for Provider to perform the services; (b) designate a project manager to serve as the primary point of contact for day-to-day communications; (c) review and provide feedback on Deliverables within the timeframes specified in each Statement of Work; (d) make all decisions and approvals in a timely manner so as not to delay Provider's performance; and (e) ensure that all information and materials provided to Provider are accurate and complete.

2.6 Subcontracting. Provider may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the services, provided that: (a) Provider shall remain fully responsible for the performance of any subcontracted work; (b) Provider shall ensure that each subcontractor is bound by confidentiality and data protection obligations no less protective than those set forth in this Agreement; (c) Provider shall obtain Client's prior written consent before engaging any subcontractor that will have access to Client's Confidential Information or Personal Data; and (d) Provider shall be liable for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors as if they were the acts and omissions of Provider itself.

ARTICLE 3 - TERM AND TERMINATION

3.1 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of thirty-six (36) months, unless earlier terminated in accordance with the provisions of this Article 3 (the "Initial Term").

3.2 Renewal. Upon expiration of the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods (each, a "Renewal Term"), unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal to the other Party at least ninety (90) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term. The Initial Term and all Renewal Terms are collectively referred to as the "Term."

3.3 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing one hundred twenty (120) days prior written notice to the other Party. In the event of termination for convenience by Client, Client shall pay Provider for all services performed and expenses incurred through the effective date of termination, plus any non-cancellable commitments made by Provider in reliance on the Agreement.

3.4 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if: (a) the other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice specifying the breach; (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed for a substantial part of its assets; or (c) the other Party is acquired by a direct competitor of the terminating Party.

3.5 Termination of Individual SOWs. Either Party may terminate an individual Statement of Work without terminating this Agreement, subject to the termination provisions specified in such Statement of Work or, if none are specified, subject to thirty (30) days prior written notice.

3.6 Effect of Termination. Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement: (a) all outstanding Statements of Work shall automatically terminate unless the Parties agree in writing to the contrary; (b) Client shall pay all fees and expenses due for services performed through the effective date of termination; (c) each Party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession; (d) Provider shall deliver to Client all completed or partially completed Deliverables for which Client has paid; and (e) Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination, at Client's expense at Provider's then-current rates.

3.7 Survival. The following provisions shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement: Articles 1, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, and 12, together with any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive.

ARTICLE 4 - FEES AND PAYMENT

4.1 Fees. Client shall pay Provider the fees set forth in each Statement of Work for the services performed thereunder. Unless otherwise specified in a Statement of Work, fees shall be calculated on a time-and-materials basis using the rate card attached hereto as Exhibit D.

4.2 Invoicing. Provider shall submit invoices to Client on a monthly basis, no later than the fifteenth (15th) day of each calendar month for services performed during the preceding month. Each invoice shall include a reasonably detailed description of the services performed, the hours expended by each individual, and any expenses incurred.

4.3 Payment Terms. Client shall pay all undisputed amounts within forty-five (45) days of receipt of a properly submitted invoice. Payment shall be made by wire transfer to the account designated by Provider. All fees are stated in United States Dollars.

4.4 Late Payments. Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at the lesser of: (a) one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month; or (b) the maximum rate permitted by applicable law. Provider may also suspend performance of services if any undisputed payment is more than thirty (30) days overdue, provided that Provider gives Client ten (10) Business Days' prior written notice of its intent to suspend.

4.5 Disputed Invoices. Client may dispute any invoice or portion thereof by providing Provider with written notice of the dispute within thirty (30) days of receipt of the invoice. The notice shall include a reasonably detailed explanation of the basis for the dispute. Client shall pay all undisputed portions of the invoice in accordance with Section 4.3. The Parties shall work in good faith to resolve any invoice disputes within thirty (30) days of the date of Client's dispute notice.

4.6 Expenses. Provider shall be reimbursed for reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses incurred in the performance of services, including travel, lodging, and meals. All expenses over Five Hundred Dollars ($500) must be approved in advance by Client's project manager. Provider shall submit expense reports with supporting documentation together with its monthly invoices.

4.7 Taxes. All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. Client shall be responsible for all sales, use, value-added, and similar taxes imposed on the services, excluding taxes based on Provider's income. If Provider is required to collect any such taxes, they shall be added to the invoice and paid by Client.

Subject to Section 5.2, all Deliverables created by Provider specifically for Client under this Agreement shall be considered works made for hire to the fullest extent permitted by law. To the extent any Deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire, Provider hereby irrevocably assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in and to such Deliverable, including all Intellectual Property Rights therein.

Provider retains all right, title, and interest in and to any pre-existing intellectual property, tools, frameworks, libraries, methodologies, and know-how that Provider owned or developed prior to this Agreement or develops independently outside the scope of this Agreement ("Provider IP"). 5.3 Third-Party Components. Provider shall obtain Client's prior written consent before incorporating any third-party software, open-source components, or other materials into any Deliverable. Provider shall identify all third-party components and the applicable license terms in writing before such incorporation.

5.4 Client Materials. Client grants Provider a non-exclusive, limited license to use Client's pre-existing materials, trademarks, and other intellectual property solely to the extent necessary to perform the services under this Agreement. All rights in Client's pre-existing materials remain with Client.

5.5 Moral Rights. To the extent permitted by applicable law, Provider waives and agrees not to assert any moral rights or similar rights that Provider may have in the Deliverables.

ARTICLE 6 - CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Obligations. Each Party agrees to: (a) hold the other Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence using at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement; (c) limit access to Confidential Information to those employees, contractors, and agents who have a need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those contained herein; and (d) use Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing obligations or exercising rights under this Agreement.

6.2 Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party; (b) was rightfully in the receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure by the disclosing Party; (c) is rightfully obtained by the receiving Party from a third party without restriction on disclosure; or (d) is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of or reference to the disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

6.3 Required Disclosures. A receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided that the receiving Party: (a) gives the disclosing Party prompt written notice of the requirement, to the extent legally permissible; (b) cooperates with the disclosing Party's efforts to obtain a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and (c) discloses only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed.

6.4 Duration. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Article 6 shall continue for a period of five (5) years following the expiration or termination of this Agreement, except with respect to trade secrets, which shall be protected for so long as they remain trade secrets under applicable law.

6.5 Return of Materials. Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon the disclosing Party's written request, the receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all tangible materials containing Confidential Information, and shall certify in writing that it has done so. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law or regulation, or as part of its routine backup procedures, provided that such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

ARTICLE 7 - DATA PROTECTION AND SECURITY

7.1 Compliance with Laws. Each Party shall comply with all applicable data protection and privacy laws, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and any other applicable federal, state, or local data protection laws. To the extent Provider processes Personal Data on behalf of Client, the Parties shall enter into a Data Processing Agreement substantially in the form attached hereto as Exhibit E.

7.2 Security Measures. Provider shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect Client's data and systems against unauthorized access, use, modification, disclosure, or destruction. Such safeguards shall include, at a minimum: (a) encryption of data in transit and at rest using industry-standard algorithms; (b) multi-factor authentication for all administrative access; (c) regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing; (d) intrusion detection and prevention systems; (e) access controls based on the principle of least privilege; and (f) comprehensive logging and monitoring of system access and activity.

7.3 Security Certifications. Provider shall maintain SOC 2 Type II certification and ISO 27001 certification throughout the term of this Agreement. Provider shall provide Client with copies of its most recent audit reports and certifications upon request, no more than once per calendar year.

7.4 Security Incident Response. In the event of a security incident involving Client's data, Provider shall: (a) notify Client within twenty-four (24) hours of becoming aware of the incident; (b) take immediate steps to contain and remediate the incident; (c) cooperate with Client's investigation of the incident; (d) provide Client with a detailed written incident report within seventy-two (72) hours; and (e) implement measures to prevent similar incidents in the future. Provider shall bear all costs associated with the response to and remediation of any security incident caused by Provider's failure to comply with its obligations under this Article 7.

7.5 Data Location. All Client data shall be stored and processed within the continental United States unless otherwise agreed in writing by Client. Provider shall not transfer Client data to any jurisdiction outside the United States without Client's prior written consent.

7.6 Data Retention and Deletion. Upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, or upon Client's written request, Provider shall securely delete or return all Client data in its possession within thirty (30) days. Provider shall certify in writing that all Client data has been securely deleted in accordance with NIST SP 800-88 guidelines or equivalent standards.

7.7 Audit Rights. Client shall have the right, upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice and no more than once per calendar year, to audit Provider's compliance with the security and data protection requirements of this Agreement. Such audits may be conducted by Client or a qualified third-party auditor designated by Client, at Client's expense. Provider shall cooperate fully with any such audit and shall promptly remediate any deficiencies identified.

ARTICLE 11 - DISPUTE RESOLUTION

11.1 Escalation. The Parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement through informal negotiations. Either Party may initiate the dispute resolution process by sending a written notice to the other Party describing the dispute in reasonable detail. Within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of such notice, each Party shall designate a senior executive with authority to resolve the dispute, and such executives shall meet (in person or by videoconference) within twenty (20) Business Days to attempt to resolve the dispute.

11.2 Mediation. If the dispute is not resolved through informal negotiations within forty-five (45) days of the initial dispute notice, either Party may submit the dispute to mediation administered by JAMS in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. The mediation shall be conducted by a single mediator selected by mutual agreement of the Parties, or if the Parties cannot agree, by JAMS in accordance with its procedures. Each Party shall bear its own costs of mediation, and the Parties shall share equally the fees and expenses of the mediator.

11.3 Arbitration. If the dispute is not resolved through mediation within sixty (60) days of the submission to mediation, either Party may submit the dispute to binding arbitration administered by JAMS in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, in accordance with JAMS Comprehensive Arbitration Rules and Procedures. The arbitration shall be conducted by a panel of three (3) arbitrators, with each Party selecting one arbitrator and the two Party-selected arbitrators selecting the third. The arbitrators shall have expertise in technology and commercial disputes. The arbitration award shall be final, binding, and enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction.

11.4 Injunctive Relief. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief from any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm, including but not limited to the unauthorized disclosure of Confidential Information or infringement of Intellectual Property Rights.

11.5 Continued Performance. Pending the resolution of any dispute, the Parties shall continue to perform their respective obligations under this Agreement, except to the extent such obligations are the subject of the dispute.

EXHIBIT B - SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT

1. System Availability
Provider shall ensure that all hosted services maintain an availability of at least 99.9% measured on a monthly basis, excluding scheduled maintenance windows. Scheduled maintenance shall be performed during off-peak hours (between 12:00 AM and 6:00 AM Eastern Time on weekends) with at least seventy-two (72) hours advance notice to Client.

2. Response Times
Critical incidents (system down or major functionality impaired): Response within fifteen (15) minutes, resolution within four (4) hours.
High-priority incidents (significant functionality impaired): Response within one (1) hour, resolution within eight (8) hours.
Medium-priority incidents (minor functionality impaired): Response within four (4) hours, resolution within two (2) Business Days.
Low-priority incidents (cosmetic or informational): Response within one (1) Business Day, resolution within five (5) Business Days.

3. Performance Standards
All web applications shall have a page load time of less than two (2) seconds under normal operating conditions. API endpoints shall respond within five hundred (500) milliseconds for ninety-five percent (95%) of requests. Batch processing jobs shall complete within the scheduled window with a success rate of at least ninety-nine percent (99%).

4. Service Credits
If Provider fails to meet the availability targets set forth above, Client shall be entitled to service credits as follows: availability between 99.0% and 99.9% shall entitle Client to a credit of five percent (5%) of the monthly fees; availability between 98.0% and 99.0% shall entitle Client to a credit of ten percent (10%); availability below 98.0% shall entitle Client to a credit of twenty percent (20%). Service credits shall be applied to the next invoice and shall not exceed twenty percent (20%) of the monthly fees in any given month. Service credits are Client's sole and exclusive remedy for Provider's failure to meet the availability targets.
